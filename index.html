Reactive Tales

A tale should be reactive in nature, i.e. it should alter itself in meaningful ways by responding / reacting to readers' actions or sorroundings (e.g. Is it evening? Is it a winter? What is the location? Is it known to be haunted? etc). So, a Reactale (or Reactive Tale) is a tale which refuses to stay the same and dead as it was when created, but rather it aspires to be alive, to interact and to transform.

Reactive Branching

Every story starts the same i.e. flat and single piece. Then as we read it, at some point we start thinking, hey! why did the character do that? What would have happened if he had done something different. And thus we create a branch of our own in our mind. Reactale encourages authors to anticipate those alternate flows and offer it to the readers. Thus readers will initially see only a part (storylet) of the story, followed by some choices. And depending on the choice they select, will then unlock the next part (another storylet).
